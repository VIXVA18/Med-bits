Benadryl (Dextromethorphan)

Benadryl is a well-known over-the-counter medication commonly used for the relief of cough and cold symptoms. Its primary active ingredient, dextromethorphan, acts as a cough suppressant.

Uses :

Cough Suppression: Relieves dry, irritating coughs.Cold Relief: Alleviates symptoms associated with common colds.

Dosage and Administration :

Adults and Children over 12 years:

Typically, 10-20 mg every 4 hours or 30 mg every 6-8 hours as needed. Do not exceed 120 mg in 24 hours.

Children 6 to 12 years:

Usually, 5-10 mg every 4 hours or 15 mg every 6-8 hours. Do not exceed 60 mg in 24 hours.Children under 6 years: Not recommended without medical advice.

Side Effects

Common: Drowsiness, dizziness, nausea, and vomiting.Serious (Rare): Severe allergic reactions, confusion, and hallucinations (especially if abused).

Precautions

Allergies: Avoid if you have an allergy to dextromethorphan or any other ingredients in the formulation.

Medical Conditions: Consult a doctor if you have a history of chronic bronchitis, asthma, or any other chronic lung disease.

Drug Interactions: Be cautious when using with other CNS depressants or MAO inhibitors, as it may cause severe interactions.

Pregnancy and Breastfeeding: Use only if clearly needed and prescribed by a healthcare provider.

Brufen (Ibuprofen)

Brufen: A brand name for ibuprofen, a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID).

Uses :

Pain Relief: Effective for mild to moderate pain such as headaches, menstrual cramps, toothaches, and muscle aches.

Inflammation Reduction: Used in conditions like rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, and other musculoskeletal disorders.

Fever Reduction: Helps in lowering fever.

Postoperative Pain: Used to manage pain after surgery.

Dosage and Administration :

Adults:

Mild to Moderate Pain: 200-400 mg every 4-6 hours as needed.Inflammation (Arthritis): 400-800 mg three to four times daily.Maximum Daily Dose: Should not exceed 3200 mg.

Children (varies by weight):

Fever and Pain: 10 mg/kg every 6-8 hours.Maximum Daily Dose: 40 mg/kg.

Administration:Oral tablets, capsules, chewable tablets, or liquid suspension.Take with food or milk to minimize gastrointestinal discomfort.

Side Effects

Common: Headache, dizziness, or drowsiness.

Precautions

Pre-existing Conditions: Caution in patients with cardiovascular disease, hypertension, gastrointestinal disorders, liver or kidney impairment.

Allergic Reactions: Avoid if there's a history of hypersensitivity to ibuprofen, aspirin, or other NSAIDs.

Drug Interactions: Can interact with anticoagulants, other NSAIDs, corticosteroids, certain antihypertensives, and diuretics.

Pregnancy and Lactation: Avoid during the third trimester; consult a doctor during breastfeeding.

Alcohol Consumption: Limit alcohol intake to reduce the risk of gastrointestinal bleeding.

Calpol (Paracetamol)h1>

Brufen: A brand name for paracetamol (also known as acetaminophen in the U.S.)..

Uses :

Pain Relief: Effective for mild to moderate pain such as headaches, muscle aches, toothaches, and menstrual cramps.

Fever Reduction: Used to reduce fever associated with colds, flu, or infections.

Post-vaccination Fever: Commonly used in children to manage fever after vaccinations.

Dosage and Administration :

Adults:

Standard Dose: 500 mg to 1,000 mg every 4-6 hours as needed.Maximum Daily Dose: Should not exceed 4,000 mg.

Children (varies by weight):

General Dose: 10-15 mg/kg every 4-6 hours as needed.Maximum Daily Dose: Typically up to 60 mg/kg.

Administration:Available in tablets, capsules, liquid suspension, and suppositories.Can be taken with or without food.

Side Effects

Common: Generally well-tolerated with few side effects.

Serious: Rare but can include allergic reactions like rash, itching, or swelling.Hepatotoxicity in cases of overdose, which can lead to liver failure.

Precautions

Pre-existing Conditions: Caution in patients with cardiovascular disease, hypertension, gastrointestinal disorders, liver or kidney impairment.

Allergic Reactions: Avoid if there's a history of hypersensitivity to ibuprofen, aspirin, or other NSAIDs.

Drug Interactions: Can interact with anticoagulants, other NSAIDs, corticosteroids, certain antihypertensives, and diuretics.

Pregnancy and Lactation: Avoid during the third trimester; consult a doctor during breastfeeding.

Alcohol Consumption: Limit alcohol intake to reduce the risk of gastrointestinal bleeding.
